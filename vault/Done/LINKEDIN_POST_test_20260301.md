## Post Content

I've been building something quietly for the past few weeks — a **Personal AI Employee** that runs 24/7 on my own machine.

It watches my Gmail, LinkedIn messages, and social feeds. It triages what matters, drafts responses for my review, and never acts without my sign-off.

Here's what surprised me most: the hardest part wasn't the AI. It was the **workflow design**.

Inbox → Needs_Action → Pending_Approval → Approved → Done

That one pipeline — with a human checkpoint before every external action — is what makes it trustworthy enough to actually delegate to.

A few things I learned building it:

→ Local-first beats cloud-first for personal automation. No SaaS fees, no data leaving your machine.

→ "Human-in-the-loop" isn't a limitation — it's the feature. I approve every email before it sends.

→ Structured vault folders beat a database. Plain markdown files that I can read, edit, and version-control.

→ Circuit breakers matter. When a watcher crashes 3× in 5 minutes, it pauses itself and files an alert instead of spamming errors.

It's not AGI. It's closer to a very organised, tireless assistant who never forgets to file things and always asks before acting.

If you're building anything similar or thinking about personal automation — I'd love to compare notes.
